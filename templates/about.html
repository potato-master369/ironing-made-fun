Introduction

  • Ironing is the use of an iron, usually heated, to remove wrinkles and unwanted creases from fabric. The heating is commonly done to a temperature of 180–220 °C (360–430 °F), depending on the fabric. Ironing works by loosening the bonds between the long-chain polymer molecules in the fibres of the material.
  • Ironing is the process of using a hot iron to remove wrinkles and creases from clothes, especially for professional and formal occasions like dress shirts, dress pants, and blouses. Some looser clothes don't require ironing. While some people opt for dry cleaning, mastering ironing techniques at home with an iron, ironing board, and spray bottle is possible.
